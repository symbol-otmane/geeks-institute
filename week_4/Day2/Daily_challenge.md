## Level 2: Analytical Byte 

**Challenge:** "The Evolution Investigator"

### Option A: 3D Graphics and FPS Evolution
- The emergence of **3D graphics** enabled fully navigable, immersive environments for FPS games.  
- **Key Game Reference:** *Quake (1996)*  
- **Impact:** Allowed verticality, strafing, complex level design, and multiplayer arenas, significantly increasing FPS popularity and innovation.

### Option B: Permadeath & Procedural Generation in Roguelites
- **Permadeath:** Failure has tangible consequences, making each run meaningful.  
- **Procedural Generation:** Levels are unpredictable, maintaining replayability.  
- **Impact on Progression:** Unlike traditional linear games, progression emphasizes **adaptation, learning, and meta-progression**.  
- **Example:** *Hades* – persistent upgrades soften permadeath while maintaining challenge.

## Level 3: Design & Influence Deep Dive

**Challenge:** "The Game Designer's Dilemma"

- **Technological Advancement:** 3D graphics engines and networked online connectivity enabled GTA III’s open-world and WoW’s persistent online world.

- **Community Trend (WoW):** Social collaboration and guild systems encouraged cooperative play, emergent social dynamics, and long-term engagement.

- **Proposed Game Idea:**  
  - **Title Concept:** *Urban Legends Online*  
  - **Core Genre:** Open-World MMORPG / Action-Adventure Hybrid  
  - **Concept:**  
    - Sandbox city with GTA-style freedom.  
    - Persistent online world for guilds, cooperative missions, and dynamic events (WoW-style).  
    - Real-time social and economic systems; player-driven consequences.  
  - **Player Experience:**  
    - Combines freedom of exploration with deep social interaction.  
    - Emergent gameplay where player actions influence the world.  
    - Encourages collaboration, competition, and long-term engagement.
